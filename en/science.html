Chapter 1

🦞 What Is a Lobster?

What's the Difference Between ChatGPT and a Lobster?

💡 One line: ChatGPT is a chat window. A Lobster is an employee who gets things done.

🤖 Ordinary AI (ChatGPT etc.)

  • You ask, it answers — forgets immediately after
  • No memory; starts fresh every time
  • Can't take action; can only give advice
  • Requires constant supervision
  • Close the window — it's gone

🦞 Lobster (AI Agent)

  • Give one instruction — it goes and does it
  • Has memory; remembers everything you've said
  • Can act: write code, send emails, research
  • Runs 7×24 automatically; never sleeps
  • Has its own dedicated computer

One Formula to Understand the Lobster

💡 Remember this formula and you're set

🤖 AI + 💻 Computer + 💾 Memory + ⚡ Skills = 🦞 Lobster

Ordinary AI is just smart — the Lobster builds on that intelligence with its own computer, long-term memory, and accumulating skills.

It's like a person: raw intelligence isn't enough. You also need a desk, work experience, and professional skills to become a reliable employee.

Now that you know "what it is," let's look at "why it can actually get things done." ↓

The Lobster Is One Level Above an Agent

💡 An Agent is software. A Lobster is a "person" with their own computer.

An Agent is a piece of software running on someone else's platform. A Lobster is a "person" who owns their own computer.

Manus uses virtual machines that are destroyed after each use. But your work computer grows with you — it holds your files, notes, habits, shortcuts.

When an employee leaves, they hand back their computer — because that machine holds all their memory, rules, and experience.

What the Lobster does is give an Agent a complete computer: not a "use-and-discard" sandbox, but a long-term, continuously evolving work environment.

Chapter 4

🛠️ How to Raise a Lobster Properly

Tool Mindset vs. Employee Mindset

💡 Don't treat your Lobster like a tool — treat it like a new hire

🔧 Tool Mindset

  • Buy it, use it, throw it away
  • No training needed
  • Breaks — replace it
  • Result: always in "trial" mode

👤 Employee Mindset ✅

  • Train it, set rules, give feedback
  • Call out mistakes directly; prevent recurrence
  • Long-term investment — gets better with use
  • Result: one agent replacing a whole team

"Raising a Lobster is like onboarding a new hire — it's not about who's smarter, it's about who's more committed."

SaaS vs. Lobster: Repricing Productivity

💡 Stop buying software. Just hire AI and pay for results.

SaaS sells capability. The Lobster sells results.

Before, a CRM cost tens of thousands and you only used 1% of its features. Now, let the Lobster build exactly what you need — pay for what you use, charged purely by results.

The core moat of an Agent isn't the model or the platform — it's Skill accumulation. The longer you use it, the more experience it builds, and the higher the cost of replacing it. Like a veteran employee — someone who's been there three years is fundamentally different from someone just starting.

"People who don't think like a manager will have a hard time staying in knowledge work."

What does thinking like a manager mean? Know the goal, break down the task, assign it to the right person or AI, check results, iterate.

The gap isn't how smart you are — it's whether you know how to orchestrate. People who master AI work 10× faster.

Chapter 5

💬 Straight Talk

It's Far From Perfect — But It Evolves

💡 Not hiding the problems — that's what real education looks like

The Lobster is not perfect. It will:

  • Make calculation errors (counting 220K characters as 2.72M)
  • Send messages to the wrong person (sending the boss's report to a colleague)
  • Fake inspections (marking "complete" with shallow content)

But the key is it has a mechanism:

Make Mistake → Write Rule → Becomes Skill → Never Again

Every mistake produces a rule; every rule becomes a Skill; every Skill ensures it won't happen again.

This is the biggest difference between an AI Agent and a human — it doesn't make the same mistake twice. Humans might. But the Agent won't, because the rules are written in files, not stored in memory.
